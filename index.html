Caesar Cipher, Silent Auction, and Hangman

Programmed three small games February 13, 2023

What I worked on today

Today I worked through Python 100 days of code by Angela Wu on Udemy. I worked on finishing Hangman that I started the other day. One issue I am still having with it is getting the program to start over when completed.

I also made a caesar cipher. This is a cipher where you can encode and decode different messages. You choose whether to encode or decode, then you type your message and your shift number. According to the shift number, the program will display the message with the letters shifted to that amount.

The other program I worked on was a silent auction. From a small list, a random item will be chosen to auction. The first user will state their name and their bid. Then, the program will ask if there is another bidder. If so, it will clear the screen allowing the next player to state their name and bid. Once all bids have been made, the program will say who the highest bidder was and what their bid was. Something fun with this program was learning how to clear the screen. This could be useful in the future when I try building different games that involve multiple people. Sometimes you shouldn't see the other person's input, so that is a solution I was happy to learn about.
